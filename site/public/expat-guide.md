Moving to Costa Rica is one of the best decisions you can make. ## 1. What to Bring from Home

Some things are significantly more expensive or harder to find in Costa Rica. Bring them if you can:

- **Electronics** — Laptops, tablets, quality headphones, smart home devices. Tariffs make electronics noticeably pricier here.
- **Prescription medications** — Bring a 3–6 month supply and the prescriptions. Many medications are available, but specific brands may not be.
- **Specialty clothing** — Good hiking boots, technical outdoor gear, professional dress clothes in larger sizes. Costa Rica excels at casual and tropical wear, but specialty sizes can be limited.
- **Tools and hardware** — Quality tools, if you work with your hands. Bring what you rely on.
- **Books and media** — Physical books in English are expensive. Digital is fine here, but your favorites are worth the luggage space.
- **Sentimental items** — Art, photos, heirlooms. These can't be replaced, and shipping them later is costly.

**What not to bother with:** Basic clothing, shoes in common sizes, household cleaning supplies, most kitchen staples, and anything IKEA-equivalent (Cemaco and EPA are solid local options).

## 2. What to Store vs. Ship vs. Sell

This is the most consequential decision expats make — and the one most people get wrong.

**Store it** when:
- You're not 100% sure you're staying permanently
- The item has sentimental or financial value but won't survive a humid climate without protection
- You want to keep your options open for a return visit or eventual repatriation

**Ship it** when:
- You're committed to staying and the item is essential to your daily life
- It's a high-value item that would cost more to replace than to ship (specialty furniture, professional equipment, musical instruments)
- Shipping cost is less than 50% of replacement value

**Sell it** when:
- You could replace it in Costa Rica for less than the hassle of storing or shipping
- It's large furniture that won't fit a Tico-scale home
- You're downsizing intentionally — Costa Rica tends to encourage a simpler lifestyle

**The honest math:** A 20-foot shipping container from the US costs $2,500–$5,000 and takes 6–8 weeks. A storage unit in San José from $50/month gives you time to decide without pressure.

## 4. Climate Considerations for Your Belongings

Costa Rica's climate is beautiful — and merciless to unprepared belongings. The Central Valley (where San José sits) is mild, but humidity is a real factor.

**What's at risk:**
- **Paper and books** — Will mold in a damp environment. Store in sealed plastic bins.
- **Wood furniture** — Solid hardwoods fare better than particle board, which swells and warps.
- **Electronics** — Humidity + dust = corrosion over time. Use silica gel packs.
- **Leather** — Molds easily. Clean before storing, use a conditioner.
- **Artwork and photographs** — Frame under glass and store vertically. Avoid stacking.
- **Clothing** — Pack in vacuum-seal bags with cedar blocks. Avoid cardboard boxes.

**What's fine:** Metal, plastic, ceramics, glass, sealed containers, most sporting equipment.

A climate-controlled unit is the safest option for sensitive items. Ask about our unit options if you have specific concerns — we're happy to advise.

## 5. Checklist: Preparing Your Storage Unit

Before you move anything in, take 20 minutes to do this right:

- [ ] **Clean everything** before storing — especially fabric, leather, and kitchen items
- [ ] **Photograph high-value items** for insurance records
- [ ] **Wrap fragile items** in bubble wrap or moving blankets
- [ ] **Use sturdy plastic bins** instead of cardboard — cardboard absorbs moisture
- [ ] **Add silica gel packets** to electronics boxes and clothing containers
- [ ] **Label every box** on the side (not the top) so you can read them when stacked
- [ ] **Stack heaviest items on the bottom**, lightest on top
- [ ] **Leave an aisle** down the center of your unit so you can reach the back
- [ ] **Keep an inventory list** — a simple spreadsheet or phone note is enough
- [ ] **Bring your own padlock** — a quality disc lock is worth it

If you're storing a vehicle or large equipment, call us first — we can advise on the best unit size and any specific requirements.
